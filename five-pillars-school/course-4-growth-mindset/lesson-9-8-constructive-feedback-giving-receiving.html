üéØ The SBI Model for Giving Feedback

Structure feedback for maximum clarity and impact:

üìç Situation

Describe the specific context where the behavior occurred

Example: "During yesterday's team meeting..."

üé¨ Behavior

Explain the specific, observable action without interpretation

Example: "You interrupted three times when others were speaking..."

üí• Impact

Share the effect of the behavior on you, the team, or outcomes

Example: "This made team members hesitant to share their ideas..."

üëÇ Receiving Feedback with Growth Mindset

Transform all feedback into learning opportunities:

  1. Listen Fully: Resist the urge to interrupt or defend immediately
  2. Ask Questions: Seek specific examples and clarification
  3. Find the Gift: Look for valuable insights even in poorly delivered feedback
  4. Thank the Giver: Acknowledge the courage it takes to give feedback
  5. Reflect Before Acting: Take time to process emotional responses
  6. Follow Up: Share what you learned and actions you're taking

üå± Creating Feedback-Rich Environments

Build culture and systems that encourage continuous feedback:

  • Regular Check-ins: Weekly or bi-weekly feedback exchanges
  • Feedback Requests: Proactively ask for specific feedback
  • Model Vulnerability: Share your own growth areas and mistakes
  • Celebrate Learning: Recognize when people act on feedback
  • Practice Sessions: Role-play difficult feedback conversations
