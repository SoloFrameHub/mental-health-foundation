Protect your family's mental health during economic challenges

Financial stress significantly predicts mental health challenges. Economic hardship correlates with increased depression, anxiety, family conflict, and childhood behavioral problems through chronic stress, reduced resources, increased tension, and uncertainty about basic needs.

Research Foundation

Families maintaining open age-appropriate communication about finances, emphasizing unity, and implementing stress management significantly buffer children from negative effects. Children absorb parental anxiety while lacking cognitive development to contextualize economic challenges.
